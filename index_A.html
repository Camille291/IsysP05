Reviews & Recommendations

Dr POULIQUEN Cardiologist, Hospital Practitioner, teaching hospital Brest

"In my specialty, cardiology, telemedicine has an important place in the care of patients. Initially developed for the follow-up of patients implanted with an automatic defibrillator, a pacemaker, it is now also used to ensure part of the follow-up of patients treated for chronic heart failure. The eligibility of patients for remote monitoring and the terms of the latter are governed by legislative texts, and allow, when all the conditions are met, the medical prescription and the reimbursement of remote monitoring.

In the ISYsPO5 project presented by the students, the device is not prescribed by the medical profession for the management of pathology, but it is intended for people wishing to measure certain constants and check their normality. It calls on different actors to be efficient: the user taking the step of choosing this monitoring device; providers and distributors of the device, and medical resources when detecting pathological values.

The determination of the heart rate can allow, in the event of an anomaly (tachycardia or pulse too high, bradycardia in the event of a low heart rate, or of significant variation), to alert the doctor.

Blood pressure measurement is essential for detecting high blood pressure, as well as for monitoring its treatment."

.

Alexis Hernot, co-founder of a medical start-up

“The aging of the population and medical demography makes it necessary to invest in this area. This is why France has invested 15 million euros in patient monitoring. Within medical follow-up, remote monitoring of patients is a major focus, as shown by the passage into the common law of remote monitoring of 5 chronic pathologies (diabetes, chronic renal failure, chronic respiratory failure, chronic heart failure, and for patients with implantable heart prostheses for therapeutic purposes) from July 1, 2022, by a decree issued under the Social Security Financing Act (LFSS) for 2022.”
